Overview
In Germany’s motor-insurance segment, 46 insurance groups compete, and margin pressure is high. Allianz, with 8,000 agents, had stable portfolio growth but lacked scale. 
McKinsey & Company

Problem Statement
The combination of inflation, increased claims, natural catastrophes, and a brand/offerings-complexity hindered growth and profitability. 
McKinsey & Company

Approach
Working with McKinsey & Company, Allianz:

Conducted granular benchmarking against best-in-class. 
McKinsey & Company

Introduced multi-brand strategy (core Allianz, Allianz Direct, ADAC partnership) to target varied segments. 
McKinsey & Company

Built an omnichannel offering enabling seamless digital/physical product experience. 
McKinsey & Company

Applied process-mining and systems‐thinking to streamline sales, operations, and claims. 
McKinsey & Company

Deployed analytics for pricing, fraud detection, and automated repair networks (one-click scheduling, status updates, pick-up/drop-off). 
McKinsey & Company

Impact
By end-2024, Allianz achieved policy growth more than double the market rate, maintained a combined ratio below market average. 
McKinsey & Company

Customer journey became unified across touchpoints; agents and teams worked collaboratively. 
McKinsey & Company

Conclusion
Allianz’s transformation demonstrates how a holistic, data-driven, customer-centric approach across brand, process, and technology can deliver growth and customer-delight even in mature markets.
